## Design Context

### Users
B2B decision-makers (operations directors, facilities managers, VPs) at Atlanta-area businesses evaluating recycling and waste management partnerships. They're comparing vendors, assessing compliance capabilities, and looking for a partner who feels established and capable — not a fly-by-night hauler.

### Brand Personality
**Trusted, Industrial, Grounded.** Like a seasoned partner who's been in the trenches — reliable, no-nonsense, earthy. 20+ years of experience should be felt in every design decision. The site should feel like walking into a well-run industrial operation: clean, purposeful, confident.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Premium industrial — not slick corporate, not cheap municipal. Think high-end manufacturing, architectural photography of working facilities, the weight of real materials.
- **Theme**: Mixed — dark navy sections for atmosphere and authority, warm offwhite sections for approachability and breathing room.
- **Palette**: Navy (#1B2A4A) as foundation, copper (#C4956A) as signature accent (earthy, warm, industrial), sage (#6B7F5E) as secondary natural accent, offwhite (#F9F6F1) for warmth.
- **Typography**: Instrument Serif for editorial headings (authority), Plus Jakarta Sans for body (modern readability).
- **Anti-references**: Cheap waste hauler sites (clip art, bright greens, cartoonish recycling imagery). This is premium B2B — every element should reinforce that.
- **Accessibility**: WCAG AA compliance — good contrast, keyboard nav, screen reader support.

### Design Principles
1. **Weight of materials** — Design should feel substantial, like copper and steel. Avoid anything that feels flimsy, trendy, or disposable.
2. **Earned authority** — 20 years of experience should be communicated through restraint and confidence, not flashiness.
3. **Industrial warmth** — Balance the hard industrial world with human warmth through copper tones, serif typography, and generous whitespace.
4. **Show, don't tell** — Photography and real metrics over icons and illustrations. Real business, real results.
5. **Purposeful motion** — Animation should feel mechanical and intentional, like well-oiled machinery, not bouncy or playful.
